"Simulator" vs. "Emulator"
Simulators recreate an experience from scratch using original code (eg. StepMania); emulators run the original code on platforms it was not made for (eg. running PIU Exceed PS2 in PCSX2)

What Is a Simulator, and What Does it Do?

Im terms of rhythm games, a simulator is a program that simulates the gameplay loop and mechanics of a given game. Simulators are generally free to play, and can either look entirely unique, or can visually resemble the game they're made to simulate.

Noteworthy Simulators

There are a ton of simulators out there, and it can get confusing. Here's a (roughly) chronological list of modern, commonly used ones that are worth looking at!
> Click to view the list!
  • StepMania Not Recommended
    The original dance game simulator. Simulates many different games.
  • StepF2 Deprecated
    The first to accurately recreate a somewhat modern PIU experience. Developed by xMAx.
  • StepPrime Deprecated
    The first public simulator themed after PIU: PRIME 2015. Developed by the PumpSanity team.
  • Infinitesimal Maintained Recommended
    An original theme inspired by PIU: Infinity. Uses the Project OutFox engine. Developed by Team Infinitesimal.
  • StepP1 Deprecated Recommended
    The official successor to StepF2, themed after PIU: PRIME 2015. Developed by xMAx. Deprecated.
  • StepPXX Deprecated
    The successor to StepPrime. Themed after PIU: XX 20th Anniversary Edition. Developed by the PumpSanity team. Deprecated.
  • PumpSanity Discontinued Recommended
    The successor to StepPXX. Includes PIU: PRIME 2015 and XX themes, plus an original theme. Developed by the PumpSanity team. Discontinued.
  • StepP1+ Maintained Recommended
    A decrypted and community-maintained spinoff of StepP1. Includes PIU: Phoenix scoring and other QoL improvements. Developed by rayden-61.
